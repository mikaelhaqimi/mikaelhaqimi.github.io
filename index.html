Courseworks



Year 1 Semester 1

In my first semester at UTM, I took coursework that covered fundamental topics that provide a strong foundation in coding, problem-solving, and the theoretical principles needed for my studies in data engineering. Click on the course to learn more!

Digital Logic (SECR1013)

This course was an eye-opening subject that helped me understand the foundational principles behind modern computing systems. I gained practical knowledge of binary systems, logic gates, and circuit design, which deepened my appreciation for how computers perform complex tasks through simple logical operations. The subject also enhanced my problem-solving skills by teaching me to analyze and design efficient circuits for real-world applications. This experience strengthened my understanding of the hardware-software relationship and its importance in developing reliable and efficient systems.

Technology and Information System (SECP1513)

This course gave me valuable insights into the role of IT in modern organizations. It broadened my understanding of how technology drives efficiency, supports decision-making, and enables innovation. I particularly enjoyed learning about databases and system integration, which reinforced my interest in data management. This course also highlighted the importance of staying updated on emerging technologies to remain relevant in the field.

Programming Technique I (SECJ1013)

This course was a pivotal subject in my journey as a data engineering student. It taught me the importance of writing clean, structured, and efficient code. The hands-on practice helped me build confidence in problem-solving and coding, especially when debugging and improving code quality. This subject showed me the power of logic and creativity in developing functional solutions, motivating me to explore more advanced programming concepts.

Discrete Structure (SECI1013)

Studying this course was a transformative experience as it deepened my logical thinking and problem-solving skills. I appreciated how concepts like set theory and graph theory directly applied to real-world scenarios in computer science, such as optimizing networks and designing algorithms. This subject strengthened my analytical mindset and laid a solid foundation for more advanced topics in data engineering and programming.

Integrity and Anti-Corruption (ULRS1032)

This course was a thought-provoking course that reinforced the value of ethical conduct and accountability in both personal and professional life. It encouraged me to reflect on the importance of maintaining trust and transparency in all my endeavors. I learned practical strategies to identify and prevent corrupt practices, which I believe are crucial for fostering a fair and just environment, especially in the tech industry where data ethics are paramount.
